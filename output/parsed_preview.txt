MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

Effective Date: March 1, 2024

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2024 ("Effective Date") by and between:

| Acme Corporation; 200 Park Avenue; New York, NY 10166; "Customer" | CloudBase Inc.; 4500 Tech Blvd; San Francisco, CA 94105; "Vendor" |
| --- | --- |

1.  SERVICES

1.1  Scope.  Vendor shall provide cloud hosting, managed Kubernetes orchestration, and 24/7 infrastructure monitoring services (collectively, "Services") as further described in Schedule A attached hereto.

1.2  Service Levels.  Vendor commits to a monthly uptime SLA of 99.9% for all production environments. Credits for SLA breaches are detailed in Schedule B.

2.  FEES AND PAYMENT

2.1  Annual Fee.  Customer shall pay Vendor an annual subscription fee of $120,000 USD, invoiced quarterly in advance at $30,000 per quarter.

2.2  Payment Terms.  All invoices are due Net-30 from the invoice date. Late payments accrue interest at 1.5% per month.

2.3  Fee Adjustments.  Vendor may adjust fees at renewal with 60 days' prior written notice.

3.  TERM AND RENEWAL

3.1  Initial Term.  This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for twelve (12) months, expiring on February 28, 2025 ("Initial Term").

3.2  Auto-Renewal.  Unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least 45 days prior to the end of the then-current term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one-year periods on the same terms and conditions.

4.  CONFIDENTIALITY

Each party agrees to hold the other party's Confidential Information in strict confidence and not disclose it to third parties without prior written consent.

5.  INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

All pre-existing intellectual property of each party remains the sole property of that party. No license is granted except as expressly stated herein.

6.  LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

6.1  Liability Cap.  In no event shall either party's cumulative liability arising out of or related to this Agreement exceed the total fees paid or payable by Customer in the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the claim.

6.2  Exclusion of Consequential Damages.  Neither party shall be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages, even if advised of the possibility thereof.

7.  INDEMNIFICATION

Each party ("Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party from and against any third-party claims arising from the Indemnifying Party's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

8.  TERMINATION

8.1  Termination for Convenience.  Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon 30 days' prior written notice to the other party.

8.2  Termination for Cause.  Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within 15 days of receiving written notice thereof.

9.  GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of law provisions. Any disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in New York, NY under the rules of JAMS.

10.  GENERAL

10.1  Entire Agreement.  This Agreement, together with all Schedules, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter.

10.2  Amendments.  This Agreement may only be amended by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

10.3  Notices.  All notices shall be delivered to the addresses listed on the signature page below.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the Effective Date.

STATEMENT OF WORK

Intelligent Document Processing Automation

SOW Number: ENT-SOW-2024-019  |  Version 1.0  |  September 5, 2024

| CLIENT; NovaBridge Financial Services Pvt. Ltd.; 12th Floor, One BKC; Bandra Kurla Complex, Mumbai 400051 | VENDOR; Ententia AI Solutions Pvt. Ltd.; WeWork Berger Delhi One, Sector 16B; Noida 201301 |
| --- | --- |

This Statement of Work is entered into under the Master Services Agreement dated June 1, 2024 between Client and Vendor.

1.  PROJECT OVERVIEW

NovaBridge processes over 15,000 loan application documents per month — including income proof, KYC documents, bank statements, and property papers — largely through manual review. Vendor will design and deploy an AI-powered Intelligent Document Processing (IDP) system to automate extraction, classification, and validation of these documents, reducing manual processing time by at least 70%.

2.  SCOPE OF WORK

2.1  Document Ingestion & Pre-processing

Scalable ingestion pipeline supporting PDF, JPEG, PNG, and TIFF formats

OCR for scanned documents using best-in-class models

Document quality assessment and rejection workflow for illegible inputs

2.2  Document Classification Engine

Multi-class classifier supporting: Income Proof, Bank Statement, KYC – Aadhaar, KYC – PAN, Property Document, Loan Agreement, Other

Target classification accuracy: ≥95% on held-out test set

2.3  Intelligent Field Extraction

Named entity recognition and structured extraction for each document class

Extraction confidence scoring with human-review queue for low-confidence outputs

2.4  Validation & Business Rules Engine

Cross-document consistency checks (e.g. name on PAN matches name on bank statement)

Configurable rule engine for client-specific validation logic

Exception flagging and audit log

2.5  Integration & API Layer

REST API for integration with Client's Nucleus Software Finnone loan origination system

Webhook support for real-time processing callbacks

Admin dashboard for monitoring throughput, accuracy metrics, and queue status

2.6  Testing, UAT & Handover

Unit and integration test suite with ≥80% code coverage

User Acceptance Testing support (2 rounds)

Technical documentation, runbooks, and 2-day on-site training session in Mumbai
